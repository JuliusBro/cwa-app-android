Basic calculation of the Risk Score

The risk score is calculated using the data collected by the App like this: val riskScore = (maximumRiskScore / normalizationDivisor) * weightedAttenuationDuration

Where

  • maximumRiskScore is the time with the highest risk in the used data set
  • normalizationDivisor is a constant, that - when divided by the maximumRiskScore - changes the calculated time according to the classification by the maximumRiskScore
    • If the risk of maximumRiskScore is classified as average, the calculated time is not changed
    • If the risk of maximumRiskScore is classified as above average, the calculated time is multiplied by 1.5
    • If the risk of maximumRiskScore is classified as below average, the calculated time divided by 6

  • weightedAttenuationDuration is the sum of the times that the person was in low/average/high risk areas, after those times have been multiplied by constant weights

      val weightedAttenuationDuration =
      weightedAttenuationLow
          .plus(weightedAttenuationMid)
          .plus(weightedAttenuationHigh)
          .plus(defaultBucketOffset)

  val weightedAttenuationLow =
      attenuationParameters.weights.low.capped()
          .times(exposureSummary.attenuationDurationsInMinutes[0])
  val weightedAttenuationMid =
      attenuationParameters.weights.mid.capped()
          .times(exposureSummary.attenuationDurationsInMinutes[1])
  val weightedAttenuationHigh =
      attenuationParameters.weights.high.capped()
          .times(exposureSummary.attenuationDurationsInMinutes[2])


    This code can be found in the class RiskLevelCalculation in the package risk.

Details about the Risk Score

The classification of periods into low, mid and high is done according to the distance between sender and receiver, where a distance above 3 meters is considered low, a distance below 1.5 meters is considered high and a distance between 1.5 meter and 3 meters is considered mid. Periods shorter than 10 minutes and distances above 10 meters are always discarded

There are no further increments of the result. The Risk Score is simply the weighted time periods. If these are above 15 minutes, the user is classified as increased risk. In that case, he will be notified and further actions are recommended.
